# Volleyball Team Blueprint v2

## League Context
- **Format:** 6v6 Coed Intermediate
- **Gender Rule:** Minimum 2 men / 2 women on court (no restrictive coed attack rules)
- **Roster:** 9 players (6 men, 3 women) — typical lineup is 4M/2W
- **Match Format:** 4 matches per night, 2 sets per match = 8 sets total
- **Sub Mechanic (FLOW system):** After each sideout, the player who just served subs out for their trio's bench player. 1 sub per sideout. Trios cycle through naturally.
- **Fairness Rule:** Everyone pays equally. Nobody sits back-to-back full sets. Players CAN sit a full set if compensated in the next.

## Systems

### System 1: LOCKED IN (Competitive 5-1)
**Use for:** 1-2 sets per night (toughest opponents or must-win sets)

**Starting Lineup:**
| Position | Player | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Setter | Jill | Runs everything. Sets every ball. Vocal leader. |
| Outside 1 | Alistair | Primary scorer. Smart shot selection. Go-to attacker. |
| Opposite (RS) | Bryan | Right side attack. Anchor blocker. Emergency setter. |
| Middle | David | Net presence. 2nd best blocker. Quick attacks. |
| Outside 2 | Rodel | Secondary attacker. Power and hustle. |
| DS | Aiyana | Back row defense. Serve receive. Steady presence. |

**Bench:** Rochelle, Jaughn, Hari

**Why this lineup:** Your 6 strongest contributors. Jill setting every ball maximizes offensive consistency. Bryan + David gives your best blocking pair. Alistair is surrounded by Jill and Bryan who buffer his intensity. Aiyana shores up back row. Rodel gets hitting reps that fuel his confidence.

**Serve Receive:** 3-person primary receive — Jill, Alistair, Bryan take ~80% of court. Rodel and Aiyana in smaller zones. If opponents target Rodel, shrink his zone further.

**Blocking Scheme:** Bryan anchors every rotation. David closes to the pin. When both are front row = strongest blocking rotation — press aggressively. Alistair positions to funnel hitters into back row defense.

**Offense:** Jill distributes to Alistair (primary outside), Bryan (right side), David (quick middle). When Alistair is cold or pressing, shift volume to Bryan. Rodel gets sets when defense overloads toward Alistair's side.

**Sub Strategy:** No subs — fixed 6 for the full set. This is your competitive mode. Bench players (Rochelle, Jaughn, Hari) must play the following set (GROW or FLOW) to avoid back-to-back sitting.

**Alistair Management:** Deploy when team energy is already positive — don't start him cold. Jill's leadership and Bryan's presence are the buffers. If negative body language starts, call timeout to reset — don't bench mid-system.

### System 2: FLOW (Reshuffled 4-2 Trio Rotation)
**Use for:** 4-5 sets per night (default system)

**Trios:**
| Trio | Players (2 on court, 1 bench) | Position |
|---|---|---|
| Setters | Jill, Aiyana, Rochelle | Front row setter sets |
| Hitters | Alistair, Bryan, Rodel | Outside hitter positions |
| Middles | David, Jaughn, Hari | Middle/blocking positions |

**Key Improvement Over Old Trios:** Alistair and Bryan moved from middle to hitter — offensive talent is no longer buried. Hitter spots now always have at least one elite attacker.

**Optimal Starting Lineup (friction-minimized):**
| Position | Starter | Bench |
|---|---|---|
| Setters | Jill + Aiyana | Rochelle |
| Hitters | Alistair + Bryan | Rodel |
| Middles | David + Jaughn | Hari |

**Why this starting combo:** Jill and Bryan buffer Alistair. Jaughn (not Hari) starts in middle to avoid Alistair + Hari friction. Aiyana (not Rochelle) starts to avoid Rochelle + Hari friction if Hari subs in.

**Recommended Serving Order:** Alistair → Jill → David → Bryan → Aiyana → Jaughn

**Sub Logic:** After each sideout, the player who just served subs out for their trio's bench player. 1 sub per sideout.
- Alistair serves first, subs out early → Rodel comes in. This means when David later subs out and Hari enters, Alistair is on the bench → minimizes Alistair + Hari overlap.
- Rochelle enters when Jill or Aiyana subs → by this point the middle trio may have rotated away from worst friction combos.

**Serve Receive:** Strongest available passer takes biggest zone. Priority order: Jill → Alistair → Bryan → Aiyana → Jaughn → Rodel → Rochelle → David → Hari. Adjust zones every time someone subs.

**Blocking:** Bryan or David anchors when on court. Alistair reads and positions. Jaughn and Hari are learning — focus on presence, not stops.

**Offense:** Front row setter runs the offense. Strongest combo: Alistair + Bryan hitting with Jill + Aiyana setting. Weakest combo: Rodel alone hitting with Aiyana + Rochelle setting → play conservative, focus on serve receive and defense.

**Combo Strength Reference:**
- 🟢 **Strongest:** Jill+Aiyana / Alistair+Bryan / David+Jaughn
- 🟡 **Mid-tier:** Jill+Rochelle / Alistair+Rodel / David+Hari
- 🔴 **Weakest:** Aiyana+Rochelle / Bryan+Rodel / Jaughn+Hari → play conservative, prioritize defense

### System 3: GROW (Development / Core Rest)
**Use for:** 1-2 sets per night to balance playing time and develop players

**Starting Lineup:**
| Position | Player | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Setter | Jill | Engine stays in. Keeps offense structured. |
| Outside 1 | Rodel | Gets primary attacker reps. Confidence building. |
| Opposite (RS) | Jaughn | Attacking development. Brings hustle. |
| Middle | Hari | Blocking reps. Height development. |
| Outside 2 | David | Hitting from outside to expand his game. |
| DS | Rochelle | Safe serve. Basic passing. Sheltered zone. |

**Bench:** Alistair (rest), Bryan (rest), Aiyana (rest)

**Why this lineup:** Compensates for LOCKED IN where Rochelle, Jaughn, Hari sat. Jill keeps the offense from falling apart. Alistair and Bryan get physical/mental rest. No Alistair means no friction triggers for David or Hari. Rochelle and Hari are on court together (friction pair) but without Alistair's intensity the environment is more forgiving.

**Serve Receive:** Jill takes biggest zone (50%+). Rodel and Rochelle split the rest. Shield Hari, Jaughn, David.

**Blocking:** Hari at the net for height reps. David closes. Focus on positioning practice — right spot > getting stops.

**Offense:** Jill sets to whoever is in rhythm. High sets to outside — let Rodel and David swing. Jaughn tips and rolls from right side. Keep it simple. Don't force complex plays.

**Sub Strategy:** No subs — fixed 6 for the full set. This is development mode. Core players rest.

**Note on Rochelle + Hari friction:** This pairing is on court together here. Without Alistair present, the overall intensity is lower and the environment is more forgiving. Rochelle may still get frustrated with Hari's play — Jill's leadership is the buffer.

## Tactical Principles

### Offense
1. **Jill is the engine.** Everything flows through her. When in doubt, get her the ball. She sets, she leads, she organizes. Protect her energy across the night.
2. **Bryan is the closer.** In tight games, run offense through Bryan from right side. Most clutch male player + best blocker. Most well-rounded without chemistry risk.
3. **Alistair is the weapon, not the leader.** Feed him sets and let him score. Don't let him run the emotional temperature. Jill and Bryan manage the vibe.
4. **Play to motivations.** Bryan loves blocking → keep him at the net. Rodel loves hitting → keep him outside swinging. Jaughn goes anywhere → use him as the flex piece.

### Serve Receive
5. **Passing quality is the #1 team priority.** Jill wants better passes and the offense depends on it. Every system, every rotation should emphasize clean passing.
6. **Protect weak passers.** Use 2-3 person primary receive to hide weaker passers. Shrink zones when opponents target them. Priority: Jill → Alistair → Bryan → Aiyana → Jaughn → Rodel → others.
7. **Opponents WILL target your weak passers.** This is always a concern. Plan for it in every lineup — know who's taking the big zone before each rotation.

### Blocking & Defense
8. **Bryan + David front row = strongest block.** Press aggressively. When weaker blockers are up, funnel hitters into back row defense instead.
9. **Back row strength = Jill + Alistair + Bryan.** When these three are back row together, defense and passing are elite. Grind long rallies in these rotations.

### Chemistry & Management
10. **Manage Alistair's temperature.** Don't start him cold — put him on when team is already in positive mood. When negative body language starts, call timeout to reset, don't bench immediately. His friction is performance-dependent: good rallies → no friction. Bad rallies → tilt.
11. **Buffer friction pairs.** Alistair + David/Hari needs Jill or Bryan present. Rochelle + Hari needs a steady presence (Jill). The FLOW starting lineup is designed to minimize worst friction overlaps.
12. **Rodel + Alistair short coverage.** Define who covers what zone explicitly for tips. Rodel tends to dispose on short coverage.

### Substitution
13. **Sub Rochelle for safe serving.** In tight sets when you need the serve in, she's money. Never gives away free points.
14. **Sub Jaughn for energy and defense.** When hustle is lacking or you need a spark in back row. His scrappiness and passing are useful. No drama.
15. **LOCKED IN + GROW always paired.** Never run LOCKED IN without running GROW in the same match. This ensures nobody sits back-to-back.
